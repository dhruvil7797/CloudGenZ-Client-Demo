## 1. Brand Essence & Domain Analysis

### Who They Are
NewLife Project Inc. is a **faith-rooted, African diaspora-led non-profit** based in Ottawa, Canada (established in Canada in 2014). Founded by **Brenda Williams**, originally from Freetown, Sierra Leone. The organization's tagline is **"Building Up Women of Virtue."**

The project was born from an evangelical outreach campaign in 1994. They have served **1,000+ lives** across West Africa (Sierra Leone, Côte d'Ivoire, Ghana) and Ottawa/Gatineau.

### Core Mission
> *"Empowering women and youth to achieve divine accomplishment and fulfil their purpose, rebuilding broken lives, and facilitating total restoration."*

### Target Audiences
1. **Immigrant women in Ottawa/Gatineau** — seeking community, language support, economic independence
2. **At-risk youth aged 15–21** — needing mentorship and career pathways
3. **Canadian donors & grant-givers** — philanthropists, corporations, government funders
4. **African diaspora community** — cultural belonging and professional growth
5. **Partners & Volunteers** — NGOs, universities, community orgs

## 3. Current Site Audit (Pain Points)

**Visual Design Issues:**
- Generic WordPress template — feels corporate and cold, not community-driven
- Monotone green/white palette fails to represent the vibrant African heritage
- Stock-style layout — no visual storytelling or emotional connection
- No clear donation CTA hierarchy — "Donate" feels buried
- Typography is generic serif/sans — no personality or warmth

**UX Issues:**
- Navigation overloaded (10+ items at top level)
- Shop and nonprofit content compete for attention — unclear primary mission
- Mobile experience is poor — large images, no responsive grid
- No impact metrics or social proof front-and-center
- Events calendar is hard to find

**Content Issues:**
- Mission statement buried below fold
- No compelling hero narrative — imagery doesn't communicate the "why"
- Programs explained in text tables — not engaging visual cards
- No testimonials or community stories prominent

## 4. Competitor Analysis

### Competitor 1: CAMFED (camfed.org)
- **Domain:** Girls education & economic empowerment in Africa
- **Design Style:** Deep editorial purple with full-bleed photography, serif headlines, storytelling-first
- **Strengths:** Emotional impact imagery, clear mission statement above fold, layered editorial layout
- **Learnings:** Use bold serif display fonts, let real photography speak, deep background colors feel authoritative

### Competitor 2: Girl Effect (girleffect.org)
- **Domain:** Girls' health, education, and livelihoods in Africa
- **Design Style:** Bold orange/terracotta + dark maroon, brutalist editorial typography (massive all-caps headers)
- **Strengths:** Unforgettable visual identity, extreme typographic confidence, clean navigation, bold CTA hierarchy
- **Learnings:** Don't be afraid of large, confident typography; bold color blocks create impact; simplicity in nav

### Competitor 3: NewLife Project (current)
- **Gap identified:** Needs its authentic African cultural identity expressed visually
- **Opportunity:** Be the most visually distinctive women-empowerment nonprofit in the Ottawa nonprofit space
