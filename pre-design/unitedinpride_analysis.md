# UnitedInPride — Redesign Strategy
**Client:** United in Pride | https://www.unitedinpride.ca/  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Analyst:** CloudGenZ Agency

---

## 1. Brand Audit

### Mission
Safe, inclusive, and affirming spaces for LGBTQ+ Immigrants, Refugees, and newcomers from countries where Queerness is criminalised. Building safety, pride, and belonging for Queer and Trans communities across Canada.

### Current Site Strengths
- Clear value proposition in hero
- Strong, joyful photography of real community members
- Logical navigation structure
- Mobile-responsive layout
- Emergency alerts section in footer (trust signal)

### Current Site Weaknesses
- Generic card-based layout (looks like a template)
- No animations or micro-interactions
- Weak typographic hierarchy
- Purple-pink gradient feels dated (2019–2021 era)
- Impact stats section feels undersized for the emotional weight of the data
- No storytelling arc — pages feel disconnected
- Footer is too sparse and not warm enough for the mission
- No visual metaphor for community/belonging/pride
- Missing personality; could belong to any nonprofit

## 2. Brand Essence (Distilled)

**Core Values:** Safety · Belonging · Authenticity · Resilience · Joy  
**Tone:** Warm, urgent, celebratory, intersectional  
**Visual Metaphor:** A spectrum of light — each person a unique wavelength, together forming a full rainbow  
**Brand Archetype:** The Caregiver + The Revolutionary

---

## 3. Competitor Analysis

| Org | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----|-----------|------------|
| Rainbow Health Ontario | Professional, strong authority, rich nav | Cold, clinical, not emotionally warm |
| PFLAG Canada | Rainbow gradient hero, family-forward messaging | Busy, dated layout, weak hierarchy |
| Egale Canada | Bold, dark, strong brand identity | Heavy JS, slow, not newcomer-friendly |
| The 519 (Toronto) | Community-event driven, vibrant photography | Navigation is overwhelming |
| ACAS (Asian Community AIDS Services) | Very specific demographic targeting | Not visually inspiring |

### Competitive Opportunity
None of these competitors lean into **intersectionality of LGBTQ+ + immigrant/refugee experience** as a design language. UnitedInPride can own this space visually — warm but urgent, joyful but with weight.

---

## 4. Target Audience

1. **Primary:** LGBTQ+ newcomers, refugees, and immigrants in Canada (often in crisis or transition)
2. **Secondary:** Allies, volunteers, donors
3. **Tertiary:** Healthcare & social workers looking for referral resources

### Accessibility Considerations
- Many users may have trauma histories → avoid harsh/violent imagery
- Language barriers → clear, simple CTAs
- Mobile-first → many newcomers use phones only
- High contrast → readability priority

## 5. Redesign Strategy

### Color Direction Options (for 7 design versions)
1. **Deep Aurora Dark** — #0D0B1F base, rainbow aurora gradients, white text
2. **Warm Sanctuary** — Cream/amber/terracotta, earthy and healing
3. **Bold Spectrum** — Near-black with vivid rainbow accent bars
4. **Soft Pastel Pride** — Lavender, rose, sky, mint soft pastels
5. **Kinetic Rainbow** — Animated gradient canvas, high-energy
6. **Clean Bento** — Pure white with colorful bento grid tiles
7. **Midnight Bloom** — Deep indigo/navy, glowing magenta/violet accents
8. **Nature Distilled** — Forest greens, sage, warm honey tones
9. **Cultural Mosaic** — Rich jewel tones inspired by flags and cultural textiles
10. **Corporate Trust** — Professional blue-purple, crisp and donation-optimized

### Typography Direction
- **Display:** Fraunces or Playfair Display (humanist, warm)
- **Headlines:** Inter or Plus Jakarta Sans (modern, clean)
- **Body:** Inter or Nunito (accessible, warm)
- **Accent:** Clash Display (bold, editorial)

## 6. Page Architecture (7–10 Layouts)

All versions include:
1. **Sticky Navigation** with mobile hamburger + Donate CTA
2. **Hero Section** — Full-viewport, photograph or gradient background
3. **Mission Statement** — Bold typographic section
4. **Services/Quick Access** — 4-card grid (Get Support, Care Pack, Resources, Stories)
5. **Featured Programs** — 2–3 program cards with imagery
6. **Impact Stats** — Animated counters with emotional context
7. **Community Stories** — Testimonial or story carousel
8. **Get Involved / CTA** — Volunteer + Donate dual CTA
9. **Newsletter Signup** — Email capture with community context
10. **Footer** — Multi-column with social, emergency alerts, site notice

### Expert-Level Components (5+)
- **Bento Grid** — Services displayed in asymmetric mosaic layout
- **Scroll-Triggered Counter Animation** — Impact numbers count up on scroll
- **Glassmorphic Cards** — Frosted glass program cards over gradient backgrounds
- **Intersection Observer Reveals** — Staggered fade-in for content sections
- **Aurora/Gradient Canvas** — Animated CSS gradient hero backgrounds
- **Parallax Photo Layer** — Hero photo with depth scrolling
- **Floating Navigation** — Nav that transforms on scroll
- **Rainbow Separator Bars** — Decorative pride flag color dividers
- **Story Marquee** — Horizontal scrolling community stories ticker
