ABOUT US

United in Pride has been Ottawa's cornerstone 2SLGBTQIA+ community centre since 1999. We provide a safe, affirming space where every member of our diverse community can find support, connection, and empowerment. Our centre operates on the unceded traditional territory of the Algonquin Anishinaabe people.

We believe in radical inclusivity. Our programs serve youth, seniors, newcomers, and everyone in between. From mental health support to legal aid, from cultural celebrations to housing assistance — we are here for our community, every single day.

Our mission is simple: no one in Ottawa's 2SLGBTQIA+ community should ever feel alone. We fight for equity, we celebrate identity, and we build the future together.

PROGRAMS

YOUTH PROGRAMS

Safe spaces, mentorship, leadership development, and social activities for 2SLGBTQIA+ youth ages 13–24. Weekly drop-in sessions and summer camps.

MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT

Free counselling, peer support groups, crisis intervention, and wellness workshops. Culturally competent care from 2SLGBTQIA+ affirming therapists.

LEGAL AID CLINIC

Pro bono legal services for name changes, discrimination cases, immigration support, and human rights complaints. Bilingual services available.

SENIOR CONNECT

Social programs, health navigation, technology workshops, and community meals for 2SLGBTQIA+ seniors. Combating isolation through connection.

CULTURAL EVENTS

Art exhibitions, film screenings, drag performances, spoken word nights, and cultural celebrations honouring our community's rich diversity.

HOUSING SUPPORT

Emergency shelter referrals, transitional housing assistance, tenant rights advocacy, and roommate matching for 2SLGBTQIA+ individuals.
