2001

Our Beginning

United in Pride was founded by a small group of activists who dreamed of creating a safe, welcoming space for Vancouver's 2SLGBTQIA+ community. What started as weekly meetups in borrowed spaces grew into something extraordinary.

15 Founding Members
2005

Finding Our Home

After years of community fundraising and incredible support, we opened our first permanent location on Bute Street. This space became a beacon of hope and acceptance for thousands.

500+ Visitors First Month
2010

Youth Programs Launch

Recognizing the unique needs of 2SLGBTQIA+ youth, we launched dedicated programs providing mentorship, support, and safe spaces for young people ages 13-24 to explore their identities and build community.

  • Weekly youth socials
  • Mentorship program
  • Scholarship fund
2015

Expanding Health Services

Partnering with healthcare providers, we began offering comprehensive health and wellness resources tailored specifically to our community's needs, ensuring dignified and informed care for all.

1,000+ Annual Health Consultations
2018

Arts & Culture Programs

Creative expression became a cornerstone of our community. We launched arts programs, hosted exhibitions, and provided platforms for 2SLGBTQIA+ artists to share their voices and stories.

  • Monthly exhibitions
  • Theatre workshops
  • Writing circles
  • Music events
Today

Our Community Thrives

Twenty-five years later, United in Pride serves over 5,000 members with dozens of programs, events, and services. We continue to evolve, ensuring our community has the resources and support needed to thrive.

5,000+ Active Members
150+ Monthly Events
25 Programs
