001 — Our Story

From Freetown
to Ottawa.
30 Years.

NewLife Project was founded by Brenda Williams in Freetown, Sierra Leone through an evangelical campaign in 1994. Over three decades, the mission expanded through Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana before establishing our Canadian home in Ottawa in 2014.

We serve immigrant women and at-risk youth in Ottawa/Gatineau — offering vocational training, mentorship, language education, and community programs that build economic independence and restore purpose.

"Empowering women and youth to achieve divine accomplishment and fulfil their purpose."

002 — What We Do

Programs &
Services

01

Youth Empowerment Program

Mentorship for at-risk youth 15–21. Building skills, knowledge and confidence to thrive personally and professionally.

02

Sewing & African Textiles

Intergenerational sewing classes using African print fabrics — building skills for self-employment and cultural pride.

03

French Tutoring

Bilingual literacy for children and adults in Ottawa/Gatineau — essential for employment and integration.

04

Women's World Forum

Monthly empowerment forum where women gather — online and in-person — to inspire, educate, and support each other.

05

Entrepreneurship Program

Comprehensive support for women to set up home-based businesses and achieve economic independence.

06

Africa Relief Program

Sending supplies and resources to communities in Sierra Leone, Côte d'Ivoire, and Ghana.

07

Internship Program

Meaningful work experience and career pathways for students and young professionals.

08

NewLife Designs Shop

African print clothing and accessories celebrating Black culture — online and in-store boutique.
